First call between POTUS and Zelensky, with POTUS congratulating Zelensky.

Vindman prepared talking points for this call, including corruption in Ukraine. Corruption was not discussed in the call

Pence calls Zelensky, congratulating him on his victory.

Williams participates in the call.

Yovanovitch told to return to the US on the next flight

April 26

Yovanovitch leaves Ukraine.

May (early)

Call between POTUS and Putin. The relevance of this call is has been left to implication and speculation. It is included here to provide a date in the case of future speculation.

May 13

POTUS meets Orban. Orban is highly critical of Ukraine.

POTUS says Pence will not attend the inauguration of Zelensky.

May 20

Zelensky inauguration. Attended by Sondland, Perry, and Volker.

Yovanovitch leaves Ukraine for the last time.

May 23

Sondland, Perry, Volker, and Johnson meet with POTUS. They stressed Zelensky was a good guy and urged POTUS to invite him to white house.

POTUS says Ukraine is a corrupt country full of terrible people. "They tried to take me down". Deeply rooted negative view of Ukraine aided by comments made by Giuliani and others.

POTUS tells Sondland (and other?) to talk to Giuliani.

May 25

Yovanovitch fired.

June (late)

Both regular and irregular channels want the two presidents to meet at the white house. POTUS wants to hear from Zelensky before the meeting.

Sondland, Volker, Perry, and Taylor are to call Zelensky. Sondland wants the call to be unmonitored and untranscribed. After the call, Volker is to meet with Zelensky on July 2 to make things clear that Volker is seeking cooperation on Investigations.

July (Unkown)

"In July, I became aware of [the] hold..." - Cooper

July 2

Meeting in Toronto on Ukrainian economics. Volker tells Zelensky in a "bi-lateral meeting" there is a problem with information reaching POTUS from Giuliani. Volker tells Zelensky to call POTUS directly to fix their relationship and assure POTUS Zelensky was willing to fight corruption.

July (mid)

Zelensky wants meeting with POTUS. The meeting is conditional upon Investigations.

Yermak wants a meeting with Giuliani to convince him that times have changed and Ukraine was worth US support, so that Giuliani would convince POTUS.

July 10

Danylyuk visits Washington for meeting with Bolton.

U.S. Participants: Bolton, Volker, Sondland, Perry, Hill, Griffith (?), Vindman, and another assistant to the president (?).

Ukrainian participants: Danylyuk, Yermak, Danyluk's advisor.

When Sondland started to speak abut Ukraine delivering specific investigations in order to secure the meeting with POTUS, Bolton cut the meeting short.

[Paraphrasing of Sondland] "In order to get a white house meeting, the Ukrainians would have to provide a deliverable which is investigations, specific investigations" - Vindman

At this point in time, the connection to POTUS isn't clear to Vindman.

It was clear there was an agreement between Sondland and Mulvaney that Zelensky would get the White House meeting if the Ukrainians undertook these investigations.

Bolton gets upset and cuts the meeting short, claiming those running the meeting should have nothing to do with domestic politics. He characterizes the meeting as a "drug deal", and tells Hill to speak to lawyers about the meeting.

Volker thinks everyone thought the comments were inappropriate. Volker doesn't think investigating Burisma is related to investigating Bidens.

Giuliani still talking with Lutsenko. Taylor hears this and tells Sondland.

Goal is for POTUS to invite him [Zelensky] to Oval. Volker, Perry, Bolton and I strongly recommend" - Sondland to Morrison.

July 25

Second phone call between Zelensky and POTUS (9am east coast). Zelensky speaks a little English (he is still learning), but mostly Ukrainian. Zelensky requests more missiles, POTUS says "I would like you to do us a favor though because our country has been through a lot and Ukraine knows a lot about it. I would like you to find out what happened with this whole situation with Ukraine, they say Crowdstrike... I guess you have one of your wealthy people... The server, they say Ukraine has it." ... "The other thing, There's a lot of talk about Biden's son, that Biden stopped the prosecution and a lot of people want to find out about that so whatever you can do with the Attorney General would be great. Biden went around bragging that he stopped the prosecution and if you can look into it... It sounds horrible."

Vindman makes talking-points for the call and is with Williams, and Morrison (others too?) in the situation room listening to the call. Taylor did not receive a record of this call.

The publicly available transcripts of this call are edited and imperfect. Some content and words have been changed and some have been removed.

Vindman had concerns and felt obligated to report this to people in the chain of command. "It was improper of the president to demand an investigation into a political opponent. Especially with a foreign power where there's at best dubious belief that this would be a completely impartial investigation. And that this would have significant implications if it became public knowledge, be perceived as a partisan play, undermine Ukraine policy, and undermine our national security."

Williams first time hearing POTUS talk about Investigations. Previously she had heard about this from Giuliani's press interviews. She notes the references to Biden are political in nature.

Corruption (broadly or generally) is not brought up or discussed, although Burisma specifically is.

"Heard from the White House, assuming president Zelensky convinces POTUS he will investigate / get to the bottom of what happened in 2016 we will nail down date for visit to Washington. Good luck!"- Volker to Yermak. This happened before the phone call between presidents.

Morrison feared how the call's disclosure would play in Washington's political climate.

2:31PM (EST?) email says Ukrainian embassy and House Foreign Affairs Committee are asking [DoD] about security assistance. 4:25PM email says the Hill knows about the FMF (foreign military financing) situation to an extent and so does the Ukrainian embassy.

August 22

(Call) Taylor asks Morrison if there had been a change in policy of strong support for Ukraine, to which he responded: It remains to be seen. Morrison also said POTUS doesn't want to provide any aid at all.

Taylor had previously claimed he would resign if strong support for Ukraine changed, and was now preparing to do so.

August 29

Ukrainians gain awareness of the hold on aid (due to politico article about it released August 28?). Taylor and Volker are unaware the hold is related to Investigations.

September 1

Zelensky meets with Pence in Warsaw.

Zelensky stars meeting by asking about aid. Pence says Ukraine has unwavering US support, and would bring it up to POTUS that night. Pence says POTUS wants Europeans to provide more support, and Ukrainians to do more to fight corruption.

Before the meeting, Taylor tells Danylyuk if the White House doesn't lift the hold on the aid by September 30, the funds would expire and Ukraine would receive nothing.

Sondland tells Yermak the aid would not come until Zelensky committed to pursue the Burisma investigation.

Sondland tells Taylor POTUS wants Zelensky to state publicly that Ukraine will investigate Burisma and 2016 election interference. Sondland says he made a mistake by telling the Ukrainians only the White House meeting was conditional upon Investigations when in fact everything is dependent upon that, including aid. Sondland says POTUS wants Zelensky in a "public box". Taylor asks Sondland to push back on POTUS and that perhaps the Ukrainian prosecutor make the announcement instead.

Taylor first learns aid is conditioned on investigations.

Danylyuk meets with Morrison, concerned about the possible loss of US support. The Ukrainians are troubled by the lack of answers to explicit questions about aid.

Zelensky forms a parliment. They have a 24 hour session. Might have pushed through many anti-corruption measures?

September 5

Taylor, Johnson, and Murphy meet Zelensky in Kyiv. Zelensky asks about aid right out the door. Johnson / Murphy say there is bi-partisan support for Ukraine, and that Zelensky not jeopardize it by getting drawn into US domestic politics.

Cooper recalls the only time a Ukrainian offical had raised the issue [of aid hold] was on this day. Was she part of this meeting? Different meeting?
